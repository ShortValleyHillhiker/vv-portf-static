On Craft

What it means to finish something properly

February 10, 2025

There’s a version of craft that’s about technique — knowing the rules well enough to follow them, then well enough to break them. That version is learnable and mostly useful.

The version I find harder to talk about is the one that happens at the end of a project, when the work is good enough to ship but not yet right. The temptation is to call it done. The client is happy. The deadline is real. And yet.

I’ve started to think of this as a kind of honesty. Not perfectionism — perfectionism is often avoidance dressed up as standards. This is more like being unwilling to pretend you didn’t notice the thing you noticed. You saw it. Now you have to decide what to do about it.

Most of the time, fixing it takes less time than the decision to fix it. The hesitation is the hard part.
